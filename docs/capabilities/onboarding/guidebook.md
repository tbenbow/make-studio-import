# Onboarding Block Selection Guidebook

Dev-time heuristics for block selection during onboarding. Distilled from compose learnings and review sessions. Not shipped to runtime.

---

## Hero Selection

- **HeroSplit** — best for service providers and portfolios. Balanced text + image works when you have a good hero photo of the person/team/product.
- **HeroImageBg** — best for local businesses, restaurants, venues. Full-bleed photo creates atmosphere. Requires a strong photograph.
- **HeroCentered** — best when no hero photo is available, or for text-forward brands (consulting, legal, finance). Relies entirely on typography.
- **HeroGradient** — best for tech/SaaS, startups. Modern feel without needing photography.
- **Hero** — general purpose with overlay. Good default when uncertain.

## Section Count

- **Sweet spot: 5-7 content sections** (excluding nav and footer). Fewer feels thin, more overwhelms a first-time visitor.
- For simple local businesses, 5 is fine (hero, features, testimonial, stats, CTA).
- For service providers, 6-7 is typical (add pricing, team, or FAQ).
- For portfolios, keep it tight — 5-6 with emphasis on visual sections.

## Section Ordering

1. **Hero** — always first
2. **Features/services** — establish what you offer immediately
3. **Social proof** (testimonials, logos, stats) — after features, never at the very end
4. **About/team** — middle of page, after establishing credibility
5. **Pricing/FAQ** — later, for visitors who are considering action
6. **CTA** — penultimate, right before footer
7. **Contact/Form** — after CTA or as final section

### Anti-patterns
- Testimonials as the last section before footer (feels like an afterthought)
- FAQ before features (answering questions before establishing context)
- Multiple CTA blocks (one strong CTA > two weak ones)
- Stats without context (place after the content that gives the numbers meaning)

## Social Proof Placement

- **After hero or after features** — these are the two strongest positions
- **Testimonial Large** after hero: immediate credibility for service businesses
- **Testimonials Grid** after features: reinforces specific claims
- **Logo Cloud** works best right after hero for B2B/agency sites
- **Stats** pair well with testimonials — place adjacent to each other

## Pricing Guidelines

- Only include pricing for: service providers, SaaS, subscription businesses
- **Never** include pricing for: portfolios (custom quotes), restaurants (use menu block instead)
- **Pricing Large** for 2-3 plans with detailed feature lists
- **Pricing** for 3+ simpler plans
- **PricingTwoCol** for clear good/better binary choice

## Block Diversity

When generating multiple variations of a site:
- Each variation must include at least 2 blocks not used in any other variation
- Vary section *purpose*, not just column count (Features Triple vs Features Grid is not diversity)
- Underused blocks that add genuine variety: Features Demo, Logo Cloud, Team, Editorial Split, FAQ Two Column

## Recipe-Specific Notes

### Service Provider
- Always include a features/services section — this is the core offering
- Testimonials are critical (social proof drives service purchases)
- Consider pricing if services have clear tiers
- FAQ reduces support burden

### Portfolio
- Lead with work, not text — Features Alternating or Features Demo showcase projects
- Editorial Split for about section (storytelling > bullet points)
- Logo Cloud for client logos adds credibility without taking space
- Keep CTAs subtle — "Get in touch" > "Buy now"

### Local Business
- Hero photo is essential — use HeroImageBg for atmosphere
- Stats work well (years open, customers served, menu items)
- Testimonials build trust for first-time visitors
- Hours and location info should be easy to find (Content block after CTA)
