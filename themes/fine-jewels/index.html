The maker

I believe jewelry should carry weight — not just in grams, but in meaning

I'm Lena, a self-taught metalsmith working from a small studio overlooking the Willamette. I started making jewelry after years as a ceramicist — drawn to the permanence of metal, the way it holds a shape forever once you give it one.

Every piece I make is formed by hand using traditional techniques: forging, soldering, stone-setting. No casting, no mass production. Just me, my tools, and the slow rhythm of making something meant to last.

— Lena

The making

From raw metal
to finished form

Every piece passes through my hands dozens of times. There are no shortcuts in handwork — just patience, heat, and intention.

i Raw metal sheets
and wire on bench

Material

It starts with ethically sourced metal — silver, gold, brass — chosen for the piece it will become.

ii Close-up of hands
hammering metal

Forming

Heated, hammered, bent, and shaped. Each strike is a decision. The metal remembers every one.

iii Soldering with
jeweler's torch

Joining

Soldering, riveting, weaving — the invisible work that makes a piece whole and wearable.

iv Polishing and
final finishing

Finishing

Filed, sanded, polished or patinated. The surface is where my hand meets your skin.
